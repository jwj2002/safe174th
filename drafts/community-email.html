Hi neighbors,

I'm writing because our community is facing a situation that affects every one of us — and it's time to do something about it.

Five development projects totaling 322 homes have been approved on NE 174th Street, a road never designed for this level of traffic. The roads have no sidewalks, no shoulders, no bike lanes, and no secondary emergency access. A 583,000 square foot warehouse is being built on NE 50th Avenue, adding 160–240 heavy truck trips per day to roads that are already failing.

This is not just about 174th Street. We are seeing unprecedented development across this entire corridor — projects approved and managed without the public interest in mind. This is short-term thinking that will have lasting impacts on the safety and livability of our communities.

We've put together a website with the full documentation: safe174th.com

Here's how you can help right now:

1. Sign the petition (by March 20)
Add your name at safe174th.com/sign. We are collecting signatures to present to the Clark County Council. Every name matters — it takes 30 seconds.

2. Report road safety concerns
If you've witnessed dangerous conditions on NE 174th Street, NE 50th Avenue, or NE 179th Street — speeding, near-misses, pedestrians forced to walk in the road, blocked emergency access — document it. Your firsthand account is evidence the county cannot ignore.

3. Contact your elected officials
We've compiled contact information and talking points for every level of government — county, city, state, and federal. Visit safe174th.com/take-action for exactly who to contact and what to say. A short email from a constituent carries real weight.

What are we asking?

Two things:

  • An independent, certified traffic safety assessment before any additional development is approved.
  • Preservation of the Mill Creek Overlay District, which was created specifically because this area has environmental constraints and only one road in and out.

We are not opposed to growth. We are asking Clark County to do its job — responsible development with safe infrastructure.

The county has the code, the authority, and the obligation. We are asking them to use it.

Please share this email and safe174th.com with anyone in the neighborhood. The more of us who speak up, the harder it is to ignore.

Thank you,
Jason
