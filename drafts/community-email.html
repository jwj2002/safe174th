I'm writing because the Clark County Council votes on Monday, April 27 on the 2025 Comprehensive Plan — and this one vote decides density and road infrastructure commitments for the NE 179th Street corridor for the next 20 years. It's a big deal for all of us who live in or near this neighborhood.

Here's the short version: the County approved 300+ homes on NE 174th Street and 2,900+ more across the NE 179th corridor without first building the roads that were supposed to support them. The November 2025 deadline for those road improvements has already passed. Major projects are years behind schedule. The corridor is failing right now — and this vote could increase density before a single road promise is honored.

This is bigger than our street. A group of neighbors has put together 179gridlock.com, which documents the full NE 179th Street corridor — 24 developer-commissioned traffic studies, 17+ road segments over capacity, 6 intersections projected to fail, and a concurrency system that keeps approving density the roads can't safely support. safe174th.com covers what's happening on NE 174th Street; 179gridlock.com covers the corridor-wide picture. The April 27 vote affects all of it.

It matters that the County Council sees us in the room. A packed hearing changes how elected officials vote. If we can fill those seats, it sends a message no written comment can.
