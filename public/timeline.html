Timeline

Running log of events, newest first
March 9, 2026
NE 50th Avenue Closes for 80 Days

Full road closure of NE 50th Avenue for sewer line installation (25 feet deep) by Rotschy Inc. Three of four arterials serving the Fairgrounds neighborhood are now closed or under significant construction. Leaves the neighborhood with only one reliable way in and out. Normal commute: 2.8 mi / 6 min → Detour: 7.5 mi / 18 min (+12 min each way). Site supervisor confirmed: no provision for emergency vehicles during closure.

March 3, 2026
Public Comment on 2026 Amended Annual Construction Program

Heidi Pozzo documents that the traffic study for the NE 174th Street Subdivision shows the intersection at NE 179th Street at NE 50th Avenue already exceeds county mobility standards — before a single new home is built. The proposed left-turn lane mitigation does not bring it into compliance. The only solution that works — a dual-lane roundabout — is unfunded and scheduled for post-2035. Compounding the problem, the Mt. Vista Logistics warehouse — a 583,000 SF speculative distribution facility with 108 truck dock doors — is under construction on NE 50th Avenue. An estimated 160–240 heavy semi-truck trips per day will flow through the same failing intersection at NE 179th and NE 50th, on a two-lane road with no sidewalks, no shoulders, and no funded improvements.

March 2026
Concurrency Methodology Analysis Reveals Staff Errors

Analysis of Three Creeks East and Four Creeks North approvals reveals staff applied the wrong LOS standard to modeled roundabout intersections and dismissed corridor-wide capacity failures without analysis. Five independent traffic studies from five different developers all find the same road segments failing — yet each project passes concurrency review. The county continues to approve development based on future infrastructure that is not planned, not funded, and would most likely require WSDOT funding to complete — leaving neighborhoods in unsafe conditions for the taxpaying residents who live in them.

March 1, 2026
Arterial Atlas Concurrency Analysis Submitted to County Council

Analysis submitted to the Clark County Council documenting how four interlocking mechanisms in the county's concurrency system make it structurally impossible to deny any development application on transportation grounds — regardless of actual road conditions. Roads classified as 4-lane arterials (1,800 vehicles/hour capacity) are actually 2-lane rural roads carrying 600–800 vehicles/hour. The county approves development based on infrastructure that doesn't exist, isn't funded, and in several cases depends on WSDOT projects the county has no authority to deliver.

2026
Forest and Creek Alliance Forming

Homeowners associations within the Mill Creek Overlay begin organizing to protect tributary creeks and riparian areas from development impacts. The alliance aims to ensure the county adheres to state and county rules protecting the creeks and the environmentally sensitive areas the county itself identified when it created the overlay.

February 16, 2026
179th Street Corridor Position Statement Submitted to County Council

Position statement requests immediate development moratorium and denial of proposed density increase in the 179th Street corridor. Cites Achen v. Clark County (1995), the foundational Growth Management Hearings Board ruling that specifically addressed the 179th Street area and found “significant inadequacies in public facilities.” The Board ruled thirty years ago that restricting development here was legally appropriate — the county lifted those restrictions in 2019 based on a six-year infrastructure commitment that has now demonstrably failed.

March 2, 2026
Neighborhood Information Log Created

This page launched to consolidate all documentation, timeline entries, and resources for neighbors regarding NE 174th Street development and safety concerns.

February 23, 2026
Concerned Neighbor Reports Gravel Manipulation and Fire Official Meetings

According to a neighbor’s account, after two meetings with Clark County Fire officials — who stated “their hands are tied by county officials” — a civil contractor placed extra gravel on the shoulder of 174th Street, after which the developer re-measured and claimed the road was 20 ft wide. This account has not been independently verified. The road is classified as a collector requiring 34 ft pavement with sidewalks on both sides.

February 21, 2026
Jason Job Follow-Up to Councilors on NE 50th Ave Closure

Jason Job sends follow-up email to Councilor Marshall (cc: all councilors) raising the NE 50th Avenue closure impact. Key question: "Does the county's obligation to approved development projects override its commitment to the safety of its citizens?" Notes three of four arterials compromised and no notification given to residents about the closure.

February 20, 2026
Councilor Sue Marshall Responds — Agrees to Pause

Clark County District 5 Councilor Sue Marshall responds to Jason Job's letter: "The lifting of the urban holding by a previous council, unleashed this rapid level of development. I agree, we should pause on new applications till the road infrastructure can catch up." She forwards the concerns to the county manager and public works.

February 16, 2026
Jason Job Sends Detailed Letter to County Council

Seven-page letter with supporting maps and data sent to all five Clark County Council members, detailing the cumulative impact of five development projects on NE 174th Street. Includes two formal asks: independent traffic safety assessment and preservation of the Mill Creek Overlay District.

February 2026
174th Street Subdivision Pre-Application Filed

Silver Buckle Homes LLC files pre-application (PAC-2025-00047) for a 103-lot subdivision on ~27 acres at 17101/16915 NE 40th Ave. This single project exceeds the county's 100-lot maximum for single-access roads. The PAC report (item 13) itself flags inadequate road access.

January 2026
Mill Creek Estates Pre-Application Filed

Ginn Group LLC files pre-application (PAC-2025-00446) for a 43-lot subdivision. This adds to the 174th Street corridor total.

July 2025
Jason Job Moves to Salmon Creek Reserve

Jason Job moves into the Salmon Creek Reserve neighborhood, one of the neighborhoods accessed by NE 174th Street. At the time, development applications for the corridor were public, but the cumulative road impact — 322 homes on a road with no sidewalks, no secondary access, and no funded improvement plan — was not disclosed in any public-facing document available to prospective buyers.

May 2024
The Columbian Reports on 174th Street Concerns

First major media coverage of the NE 174th Street development and safety concerns. Residents' objections to the M&H Subdivision are documented.

2022
Viers Subdivision (PUD) Approved

84-lot planned unit development approved (PLD-2022-00121). Dean Hergesheimer challenges approval through hearings and appeals. Hearing examiner initially rules in residents' favor, then reverses.

January 27, 2022
Superior Court Rules Against Neighbors — $17,000 Legal Fight Lost

Judge David Gregerson affirms the county’s decision under the Land Use Procedures Act (LUPA), which requires courts to defer to county expertise on technical matters. The developer’s attorney Steve Morash and county attorney Steven Archer cited county engineer David Bottamini’s May 2021 memo concluding the roads are safe. Mill Creek neighborhood residents spent $17,000 on the legal challenge.

November 2021
County Engineer Recommends 35 MPH on a 20-Foot Road

County engineer Rob Klug advises the county council to post a 35 mph speed limit on NE 174th Street — based on its proposed future width of 34 feet, rather than the 25 mph required by county code for 20-foot-wide roads like 174th Street actually is today.

May 25, 2021
Hearing Examiner Reverses His Own Denial of M&H Subdivision

Hearing examiner Joe Turner reverses his earlier denial of the M&H Subdivision (PLD-2020-00040). The reversal follows an appeal by developer Hinton Development, represented by attorney Steve Morash, who also served as a county planning commissioner. Morash argued that because 174th Street is planned for future widening to 34 feet with sidewalks, the subdivision is exempt from the county’s requirement for a second access route when more than 100 lots are served. The county accepted this argument — approving development based on a road that does not yet exist.

May 5, 2021
County Engineer Concludes Narrow Roads Are Safe

County community development engineer David Bottamini issues a memo concluding that the existing narrow roads on NE 174th Street are safe to handle all additional traffic — notwithstanding the actual conditions: a road less than 20 feet wide with no sidewalks, no shoulders, and pedestrians forced to walk in the travel lane. This memo becomes the key document cited to justify Turner’s reversal and to defeat the neighbors’ court challenge.

2020
M&H Subdivision Initially Denied by Hearing Examiner

The M&H Subdivision (72 lots, PLD-2020-00040) at 3801 NE 174th Street goes through the development review process. Hearing examiner Joe Turner initially denies the subdivision due to existing road safety conditions. This is the first formal acknowledgment by a county official that the road infrastructure is inadequate.

2009
Mill Creek Overlay District Created

After two years of community engagement, Clark County creates the Mill Creek Overlay District (CCC 40.250.060) recognizing environmental constraints and the single-access-road limitation. Sets 9,000 sq ft minimum lot size and requires additional road access before new development.
